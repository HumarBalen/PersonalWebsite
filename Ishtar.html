Ishtar Gate of Babylon (Iraq)

Ishtar Gate, an enormous burnt-brick entryway, was the grand entrance to the ancient city of Babylon, built around 575 BC. Towering over 12 meters high, this stunning gate was adorned with intricate images of dragons and bulls, symbolizing power and protection.

Why I want to go there?

Babylon is one of the most legendary ancient cities, and the Ishtar Gate represents its grandeur. The thought of walking through the same entrance that ancient Babylonians did thousands of years ago excites me. Its stunning blue bricks and mythical creatures make it feel like a portal to another time.

How to go there?

Visiting the Ishtar Gate requires traveling to Iraq. I would take a flight to Baghdad International Airport. From there, I’d head south to the ruins of Babylon, near the city of Hillah, about a two-hour drive. Since the original gate is now in Berlin’s Pergamon Museum, I would also consider visiting Germany to see it in its preserved form.
