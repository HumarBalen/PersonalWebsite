Sphinx of Giza

The Great Sphinx of Giza is a colossal limestone statue in Egypt that likely dates back to the reign of King Khafre. Measuring about 73 meters long and 20 meters high, it features the body of a lion and the face of a human, symbolizing strength and wisdom.

Why I want to go there?

I've always been fascinated by ancient civilizations, and Egypt stands out as one of the most mysterious and awe-inspiring. The Sphinx, with its enigmatic expression and historical significance, is something I’ve wanted to see in person. Standing before this legendary monument would be like stepping back in time and witnessing the grandeur of the ancient world.

How to go there?

To visit the Great Sphinx, I would take a flight to Cairo International Airport, the main gateway to Egypt. From there, it’s about a 45-minute drive to Giza, where I can easily access the Sphinx and the Pyramids. Many tours and guides are available to provide historical insights and a closer look at this iconic landmark.
