Roman Colosseum

The Colosseum, a massive amphitheater in Rome, was constructed under the Flavian emperors between 70 and 80 CE. Built on the site of Emperor Neroâ€™s former palace, it symbolized the transition from tyranny to public entertainment. It could hold up to 50,000 spectators and hosted gladiator battles, animal hunts, and dramatic reenactments.

Why I want to go there?

The Colosseum is one of the greatest architectural wonders of the ancient world. I want to stand in the same place where history was made, where warriors fought for glory and where the crowds roared in excitement. Walking through its massive stone arches would be like stepping back into the Roman Empire.

How to go there?

First, I would take a flight to Leonardo da Vinci International Airport (FCO) in Rome. From there, I could take a train or taxi to the city center. The easiest way to reach the Colosseum is by using Metro Line B, stopping at the Colosseo station*, which is right in front of the monument. Walking around Rome's historic streets would be an adventure itself!
